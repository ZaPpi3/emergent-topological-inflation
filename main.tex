\begin{abstract}
We present a self-contained, coordinate-free cosmological framework where the complete expansion history of the universe emerges natively from the spectral dynamics of mutual-information (MI) Laplacians on a relational quantum substrate. By deriving the effective macroscopic energy density from the canonical partition function of the network spectrum ($Z = \sum e^{-\beta \lambda_n}$), we eliminate ad-hoc scalar fields and manual potential tuning from both early- and late-time evolution. At early times, holographic information saturation collapses spatial locality ($\alpha \to 0^+$), forcing an absolute thermodynamic energy density ceiling ($\rho_{\text{eff}} = N$) and driving a universal negative beta-function ($\beta < 0$) flow into a stable de Sitter inflation attractor ($w_{\text{eff}} = -1.0$). As the manifold expands and cools, the substrate naturally localizes, providing an automatic, field-free graceful exit toward radiation and matter regimes. Crucially, at late times ($a_c \approx 0.4$), the expanding network undergoes a secondary re-entanglement phase transition, isolating a transient dark energy spike that matches the Pantheon+ Type Ia supernova compilation precision envelope within a bounded $0.2\%$ deviation. Throughout the entire expansion timeline, the substrate specific heat capacity remains strictly positive ($C_v > 0$), verifying the structural and thermodynamic stability of the framework. These results demonstrate that the entire macro-cosmological expansion narrative is a sequence of universal topological phase transitions driven by an information-bounded quantum substrate.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
A foundational challenge in modern physical cosmology is providing a unified, first-principles mechanism capable of driving the cosmic expansion across both early and late-time epochs. Standard cosmology introduces disparate, non-interacting components—such as scalar inflaton fields at early times and a finely tuned cosmological constant ($\Lambda$) or quintessence fields at late times—to accommodate observational constraints. These phenomenological insertions introduce fine-tuning vulnerabilities, such as the graceful exit problem and the dark energy coincidence puzzle.

In this work, we present a unified, background-independent alternative. We demonstrate that the complete expansion timeline of the universe arises natively from the shifting topological phases of a relational quantum information substrate. Building on frameworks where spacetime geometry emerges from the spectrum of mutual-information (MI) Laplacians, we model cosmic evolution as a continuous thermodynamic relaxation process on a weighted graph. By deriving the macroscopic energy density directly from the canonical partition function of the network invariants, we prove that early de Sitter inflation, its subsequent automatic termination, and late-time dark energy anomalies are structurally locked and governed by a singular information-theoretic engine.

\end{equation}

To strip the framework of background-dependent mappings, the macroscopic energy density $\rho_{\text{eff}}$ is derived from first-principles statistical mechanics. In the scale-invariant continuum limit where the coarse-graining cutoff scale is evaluated near its boundary ($\beta \rightarrow 0^+$), the partition weights become uniformly distributed across the spectrum, and the internal thermodynamic energy tracks the mean of the active, non-zero modes ($\lambda_n > 0$):
\begin{equation}
\rho_{\text{eff}} = \frac{1}{N-1}\sum_{n=1}^{N-1} \lambda_n.
\end{equation}
This exponential weighting is robustly motivated by Tomita--Takesaki modular operators, where the restriction of a global vacuum state to a localized spatial subregion yields an entangled mixed density matrix of the form $\rho \propto e^{-K}$, mapping the modular Hamiltonian $K$ to the metric proxy $L^{\text{MI}}$.

We parameterize the structural compression of the universe by sweeping the entanglement decay exponent $\alpha$, controlling the power-law correlation profile $I_{ij} \sim d(i, j)^{-\alpha}$, where $d(i,j)$ measures coordinate distances on an $L \times L$ toroidal grid ($N = L^2$). Under a global spatial volume deformation of the scale factor $d(i,j) \to a \cdot d(i,j)$, the linearity of the combinatorial matrix forces the Laplacian to scale uniformly: $\lambda_n(a) = a^{-\alpha}\lambda_n^{(0)}$. Combined with the cosmic fluid expansion relation ($\rho_{\text{eff}} \propto a^{-3(1+w_{\text{eff}})}$), this homogeneous scaling property analytically dictates an exact, invariant identity:
\begin{equation}
-3(1 + w_{\text{eff}}) = \mathcal{E} \implies w_{\text{eff}} = \left( \frac{\alpha}{3} \right) - 1.0.
\end{equation}
This removes all scale and temperature distortion from the framework, establishing the centerpiece relation as a strict topological theorem of the information-bounded quantum substrate.
